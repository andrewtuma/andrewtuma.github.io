About

I am a master's student studying applied math at San Diego State University (SDSU) under the guidance of Dr. Chris Curtis. My research is focused on dynamical systems - specifically, using machine learning and data driven decomposition techniques to understand and predict nonlinear behavior. Before this, I obtained my bachelor of science in physics at the University of Virginia (UVA). While at UVA, I studied gravitational physics under Dr. David Nichols, where I focused on finding novel methods to visualize the complex dynamics of gravitationally radiating systems while still maintaining complete representations of the spacetime.


Outside of my academic pursuits, I love spending time with my family and close friends. I come from a large family of 6 kids, where I fit in the upper middle. In descending order (age, not likeability), we are Katherine, Seth, myself, Christopher, Clara, and Ryan. I also enjoy spending time outside and playing sports, most specifically olympic weightlifting, football, rugby, and surfing.
